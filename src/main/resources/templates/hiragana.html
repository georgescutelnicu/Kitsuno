Chart

The Hiragana chart provides a foundational guide to learning the basic Japanese syllabary. Each character in Hiragana represents a specific syllable, typically combining a consonant and a vowel. These characters are used for native Japanese words and grammatical particles. Learning the Hiragana chart is essential for beginners, as it lays the groundwork for reading and writing in Japanese. By familiarizing yourself with the characters and their corresponding sounds, you'll be able to read simple texts and expand your vocabulary as you progress in your studies.

Mnemonics & Stroke Order

Mnemonics help by creating easy-to-remember images or stories for each character, making them stick in your mind. Stroke order, on the other hand, helps you write the characters more naturally and neatly. By practicing both, you'll improve your reading and writing skills while remembering the kana more easily.
