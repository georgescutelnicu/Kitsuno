Chart

The Katakana chart consists of characters used primarily for foreign loanwords, onomatopoeia, and technical or scientific terms in Japanese. Each Katakana character represents a syllable, just like Hiragana, but Katakana has a more angular and block-like style. This syllabary is crucial for writing non-Japanese names, places, and foreign words, making it an important part of mastering written Japanese. Familiarizing yourself with the Katakana chart will help you read signs, menus, and text that incorporates foreign-origin terms, which are increasingly common in everyday life in Japan.

Mnemonics & Stroke Order

Mnemonics help by creating easy-to-remember images or stories for each character, making them stick in your mind. Stroke order, on the other hand, helps you write the characters more naturally and neatly. By practicing both, you'll improve your reading and writing skills while remembering the kana more easily.
